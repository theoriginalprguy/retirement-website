Retired, Not Expired

I've spent most of my career helping organizations to be heard clearly when the stakes are high. Executive speeches, crisis statements, scientific presentations—the situations where every word mattered.

Media, messages, and the art of not panicking

For decades, my world was full of microphones, deadlines, and the quiet hum of "Don't mess this up." I worked in public relations and crisis management, writing and coaching for leaders who had to walk into the brightest, least-forgiving rooms and sound human.

It was equal parts language, logistics, and emotional triage. My job was to take complexity, pressure, and politics and turn them into something an ordinary person could listen to and think, "Okay, that makes sense."

Along the way I built creative projects, public-facing campaigns, and the occasional oddball idea that made people smile instead of cringe.

High stakes, low drama whenever possible.

Teaching people to use the voices they already have

Parallel to the corporate life, I've always taught voice—singing, speaking, and the deeper work of feeling at home in your own sound.

I've coached executives, performers, students, and people who simply wanted to stop dreading the sound of their own voice on a recording.

When it works, someone walks into a room and doesn't need a "persona." They just need to be themselves, on purpose.

Why retirement doesn't feel like "stopping"

These days, no one is handing me talking points at 6:00 a.m. The calendar is kinder. But the curiosity is the same.

The desert has become both backdrop and teacher. It rewards people who pay attention.

I'm not building a brand here. I'm building a record: of the places and people I care about, and the small, ordinary details that never make it into a press release.

If you're here, you probably know me already. This site is a way to keep you in the loop without algorithms or formal updates.
